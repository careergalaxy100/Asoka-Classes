Procedure To Enter Sainik School

Short Guide | Asoka Classes

The Sainik School admission process is based on the All India Sainik Schools Entrance Examination (AISSEE) conducted by the NTA for Classes VI and IX, followed by e-counselling and a medical examination. For Class XI, admission depends on Class X Board examination results. The final selection for all classes is based on the merit list after all stages are completed, including document verification.

For Class VI and IX

  • Apply: Register online for the AISSEE through the official portal.
  • Eligibility: Class VI: 10‚Äì12 years; Class IX: 13‚Äì15 years (as of 31st March of the admission year).
  • Written Exam: The AISSEE includes Mathematics, General Science, Social Studies, English/Language, and Intelligence.
  • e-Counselling: Conducted based on AISSEE rank.
  • Medical Examination: Candidates must qualify a medical fitness test.
  • Document Verification: After the medical test, documents are verified.
  • Final Selection: Based on the final merit list considering written score, medical fitness, and document verification.

For Class XI

  • Admission: Based on Class X Board exam marks.

Key Details

  • Application Period: Online applications open in October; exam held in January.
  • Girl Candidates: Girls are eligible for Class VI and IX in all Sainik Schools.
  • New Sainik Schools: Two routes ‚Äî 40% AISSEE candidates and 60% from approved New Sainik Schools via qualifying test.
Note: Students should regularly check the official Sainik School website or NTA portal for updated notifications, exam patterns, and important dates.

RIMC Admission Process

Step-by-Step Guide | Asoka Classes

The RIMC (Rashtriya Indian Military College) admission process involves a written exam, an interview, and a medical examination. Candidates must be studying in or have passed Class 7, be between 11.5 and 13 years of age, and submit their application through their respective state government.

The written exam tests English, Mathematics, and General Knowledge. Those who qualify proceed to the personal interview and medical test.

Admission Steps

  • Submit Application: Submit the application form to your respective state government.
  • Written Examination: Clear a written exam consisting of three papers:
    • English
    • Mathematics
    • General Knowledge
  • Interview: Candidates who pass the written exam are called for a personal interview.
  • Medical Examination: Conducted after the interview to ensure physical and mental fitness.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Age: 11.5 to 13 years old.
  • Education: Must be studying in Class 7 or have passed Class 7.
  • Date of Birth: Must be born between January 2, 2013 and July 1, 2014 for 2025 admission.

Key Requirements

  • A domicile certificate is required.
  • Passport-sized photographs must be attached with the application.
Note: Applications are accepted through state governments only. Ensure all documents are verified before submission. Visit the official RIMC website for exam dates and detailed notifications.

RMS Admission Process

Step-by-Step Guide | Asoka Classes

The Rashtriya Military Schools (RMS) admission process is conducted for Class VI and Class IX through the Common Entrance Test (CET). All admissions are strictly residential (boarding) and open to both boys and girls.

The selection is based on performance in the written test (CET), followed by an interview and a medical examination. The final merit list is prepared after all stages are completed.

Admission Steps

  • Online Application: Candidates must fill out the application form on the official RMS website and upload required documents.
  • Common Entrance Test (CET): The written exam is OMR-based and generally held in December.
  • Interview: Candidates who clear the written exam are shortlisted for an interview round.
  • Medical Examination: Conducted at designated military hospitals to ensure physical fitness.
  • Final Selection: Based on combined performance in CET, interview, and medical test.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Class VI: 10 to 12 years of age; must have passed or be studying in Class V.
  • Class IX: 13 to 15 years of age; must have passed or be studying in Class VIII.
  • Relaxation: Up to 6 months in upper age limit for wards of personnel killed in action (KIA).

Reservation / Quota

  • 70% of seats reserved for wards of JCOs/OR (including ex-servicemen).
  • 30% for wards of Officers and Civilians.
  • OBC-NCL, SC, and ST reservations applicable as per norms.
  • Girls are eligible for admission to both Class VI and IX.

Documents Required

  • Birth Certificate
  • Previous School Marksheet / Transfer Certificate
  • Caste / Category Certificate (if applicable)
  • Defence Service Certificate (for Defence Quota)
  • Identity Proof (Aadhaar / Parent‚Äôs ID)
  • Passport-size Photographs
Note: The RMS CET is usually held in December each year. Application forms are available online through the official RMS website. Ensure all documents are uploaded correctly before submission.
